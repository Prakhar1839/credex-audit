# GTM.md

## Exact Target User

**Primary:** Engineering manager or CTO at a Series A/B startup (10–80 people, $2M–$20M ARR). Not a solo founder — they feel the bill but don't have time to audit it. Not a large enterprise — no procurement process to navigate. The sweet spot is someone who controls the credit card, notices the monthly AI bill is growing, but hasn't had two hours to properly compare plans.

**Secondary:** Head of Finance or COO at the same company stage who is building a vendor spend audit practice for the first time as the company grows past 20 people.

**What they're doing right before they want this tool:** Reviewing the monthly credit card statement on a Tuesday morning. Opening Slack and seeing a "tool budget" message from their CEO. Writing a Notion doc titled "AI tools we use" and realizing they don't know what each costs. Reading a Hacker News post titled "We cut our AI bill by 60%."

## First 100 Users in 30 Days (Zero Paid Budget)

**Week 1:** Post on Hacker News as a "Show HN" — "Show HN: SpendScope — free AI spend audit for engineering teams." The framing matters: it's a *tool*, not a pitch. Good Show HNs get 200–400 upvotes. That alone can bring 800–2,000 visitors. Engage every comment personally. Credex's founder posts, not an anon account.

**Week 2:** Direct outreach on Twitter/X. Search for tweets containing "cursor.sh AND $" or "copilot billing" or "anthropic invoice." These people have already demonstrated they're aware of the problem. Reply to the 20 most engaged ones with: "built something for exactly this — [link]." No pitch. One sentence.

**Week 3:** Post in Lenny's Newsletter Slack #tools-and-resources and Indie Hackers with a genuine "I built this after noticing my own team overpaying" framing. Include a specific example: "Found that a 5-person team paying for Cursor Business + GitHub Copilot is almost certainly double-paying." Concrete examples outperform abstract value props in these communities.

**Week 4:** Cold email 30 YC companies (S23/W24 batch — visible via YC directory) whose LinkedIn shows they're hiring engineers (high AI tool spend signal). Email subject: "Noticed you use Cursor — 2-min tool." The tool does the selling; the email just gets the click.
